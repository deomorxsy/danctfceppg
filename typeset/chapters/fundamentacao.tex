\begin{example}%
\label{ex.currying}%
\index{currying}
Let $B\in\Ob(\smset)$ be any set. There is an adjunction called `currying
$B$,' after the logician Haskell Curry:
\[
\adj{\smset}{-\times B}{(-)^B}{\smset}
\hspace{1in}
\smset(A\times B,C)\cong\smset(A,C^B)
\]
